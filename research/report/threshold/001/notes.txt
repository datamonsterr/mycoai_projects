# Notes — Threshold Experiment

## Attempt 4 label bug
The dramatic jump from F1~0.09 to F1~0.45 between attempts 3 and 4 was caused by
correcting the `is_known` field encoding. Previously, unknown species were labelled
as known=1 and vice versa, which inverted the classification signal.

## Why so many FP?
The high false-positive rate (368 FP vs 172 TP in best run) is the central challenge.
Unknown species produce top neighbour scores that are nearly as high as known species
because:
- The fine-tuned EfficientNetB1 was trained only on 5 species → poor generalisation.
- Unknown species may share visual features (colony texture, colour, morphology).
- E1 strategy restricts to same environment, reducing cross-env variation but also
  reducing the diversity of the neighbour pool.

## Alternative directions not yet explored
- Train a separate binary classifier on top of retrieval features rather than threshold.
- Use top-1 species prediction confidence (softmax / temperature scaling) as signal.
- Ensemble multiple extractors (ResNet50_finetuned + MobileNetV2_finetuned) for
  retrieval before threshold.

## Formula naming reference
- `wtd_rat_halving` = 1.0*(s0/s1) + 0.5*(s1/s2) + 0.25*(s2/s3) + 0.125*(s3/s4)
- `spread_012_ov_s0` = (std(top3) / mean(top3)) / s0
- `rat01_p_rat12_m_rat23` = (s0/s1) + (s1/s2) − (s2/s3)
