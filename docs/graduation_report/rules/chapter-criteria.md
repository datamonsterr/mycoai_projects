# Chapter Writing Criteria

## Chapter 1
- Define domain terms clearly.
- Frame two core problems sharply.
- Explain why retrieval is strain-level and multi-media.
- Separate research objective and system objective.
- State scope and out-of-scope clearly.

## Chapter 2
- Organize by logical pipeline order.
- Define inputs and outputs per subsection.
- Explain settings and symbols at first use.
- Move all comparative judgments to result subsections.
- Interpret every chart/table numerically and conceptually.

## Chapter 3
- Distinguish validated implementation from prototype.
- Map system pages/workflows to actual backend/frontend behavior.
- Avoid overselling training/model-management capabilities.
- Prefer architecture, ERD, sequence, and limited evidence screenshots.

## Chapter 4
- Justify why agentic workflow is thesis-relevant.
- Provide measurable evaluation, not narrative only.
- Tie results to research throughput, quality, or reproducibility.

## Chapter 5
- Summarize actual findings, not generic project value.
- Restate limitations honestly.
- Prioritize future work from highest research/product value first.
